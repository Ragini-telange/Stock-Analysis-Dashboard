What is "Grain"?

The grain of a dataset means the lowest level of detail — the finest unit each row represents. Think of it as "what does one row describe?" In this dataset, one row = one trading day for one company. That is the grain.

ColumnGrain / Level of DetailWhat it means
DateOne calendar trading dayThe exact day the trade happened; drives all time-series analysis
Open PriceDaily — first transaction priceThe price at which the stock started trading that day
Close PriceDaily — last transaction priceThe final price of the day; benchmark for daily performance
High PriceDaily — intra-day maximumHighest price reached during the trading session
Low PriceDaily — intra-day minimumLowest price reached during the trading session
VolumeDaily — total shares tradedCount of shares that changed hands; indicates market interest

Chart-by-Chart Explanation

1. Stock Price Distribution

Shows: How often the stock traded in each price bracket (histogram). Grain: Aggregated from daily Close prices into price buckets. Insight: Reveals whether a stock spent most of its life cheap or expensive; skewness indicates growth trajectory.

2. Volume Distribution

Shows: Average daily trading volume per year per company. Grain: Daily Volume aggregated to yearly average. Insight: Spikes in volume mark high-interest years (corporate events, market rallies, crises).

3. Moving Average Trend

Shows: Quarterly average closing price as a smooth trend line. Grain: Daily Close aggregated to quarterly average. Insight: Smooths noise to reveal long-term direction; crossovers signal momentum shifts.

4. Opening vs Closing Price

Shows: Yearly average Open vs Close side-by-side. Grain: Daily Open/Close aggregated to yearly mean. Insight: When Close consistently exceeds Open, intra-day buying pressure is dominant.

5. Yearly Price Change Trend

Shows: Year-on-year % return for each company. Grain: End-of-year Close prices differenced and expressed as %. Insight: Identifies boom/bust years; negative bars mark market downturns like 2008.

6. Average Price by Company

Shows: Overall historical average closing price as a bar chart. Grain: Entire dataset collapsed to a single average per company. Insight: TCS commands the highest average price, reflecting premium IT-sector valuation.

7. Trading Volume Trend

Shows: Total annual trading volume per company as an area chart. Grain: Daily Volume summed to yearly total. Insight: Falling volume over time at Reliance reflects stock splits and market maturity.

8. High–Low Price Range

Shows: Yearly intra-day price band (max High vs min Low). Grain: Daily High/Low aggregated to yearly max/min. Insight: Wider bands mean higher volatility; Reliance 2020 shows extreme range during COVID.

9. Closing Price Trend

Shows: Quarterly last-day closing price as a continuous time series. Grain: Last daily Close of each quarter. Insight: The cleanest long-term price trajectory; TCS shows the steepest post-2016 growth.

10. Top Performing Year

Shows: The single best calendar year and return for each company. Grain: Annual — one row per company distilled to the peak year. Insight: Airtel 2003 (+359%) reflects its explosive mobile telecom growth era.

Design Rationale

Color Usage

Each company has a dedicated, perceptually distinct color — Airtel: crimson red, Reliance: electric blue, TCS: emerald green. These are consistent across every chart, enabling instant cross-chart comparison without reading labels.

Layout & Structure

A 12-column grid system organizes charts by analytical theme — distributions on top, trends in the middle, comparisons at the bottom. Wide charts span 8–12 columns for time series; compact cards span 3–4 for KPIs and highlights.

Filters & Interactivity

Company pills at the top act as visual filters. The explanation tabs (Grain / Charts / Design) allow academic readers to toggle between the data layer and the analytical context without cluttering the visual space.

Typography & Readability

DM Serif Display (headers) brings editorial authority; DM Sans (body) ensures clean legibility at small sizes. A dark background reduces eye strain during prolonged analysis. Muted secondary labels (#6b7490) create visual hierarchy without competing with data.
